Social Engineering: The "IT" Pretext

Attackers rarely start with "give me your password." They build Trust and Urgency first. By posing as IT Support and mentioning a "technical issue," they bypass the victim's natural skepticism. Once the victim provides "minor" info like a software version, the attacker uses it to pivot to more sensitive requests (like 2FA codes or remote access).
