Social Media Hacking Awareness

What is Social Media Hacking?
It is the unauthorized access to personal social media accounts by exploiting technical vulnerabilities or human psychology. Attackers use these accounts to spread malware, steal identities, and extort money.

The Risks:
A hacked account can lead to data theft, reputation damage, and financial loss. Attackers often "clone" profiles to trick your friends into sending money or clicking malicious links.

Protecting yourself requires more than just a password; it demands constant vigilance and education.

🔒 Critical Defense Tips

  • Unique Passwords: Never use the same password for Instagram and your Bank.
  • Activate 2FA: Use App-based authenticators (like Google Auth) over SMS.
  • Link Caution: If a friend DMs you a strange link, verify it via a phone call first.
  • Review Apps: Regularly check "Connected Apps" in your settings and revoke old permissions.
  • Profile Privacy: Set your profiles to 'Private' to prevent automated cloning bots.
