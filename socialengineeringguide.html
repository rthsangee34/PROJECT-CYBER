Social Engineering

Student Awareness and Safety Guide: Understanding the Art of Human Hacking

1. What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. Unlike technical hacking that targets computer systems, social engineering targets the "human factor". Attackers use deception to trick you into giving up passwords, access codes, or money by pretending to be someone you trust.

2. Types of Social Engineering Attacks

Phishing

Fake emails that look real to steal your info.

Vishing

Voice scams—attackers calling you to trick you.

Smishing

SMS or text message scams with malicious links.

Pretexting

Creating a fake scenario to ask for personal data.

Baiting

Tempting you with a "free" gift to plant malware.

Tailgating

Briefly following someone into a restricted building.

3. Social Engineering vs. Technical Hacking

Social Engineering

Focuses on Manipulating People. No coding skills needed. Hacks the brain.

Technical Hacking

Focuses on Exploiting Software. Requires deep technical skills. Hacks the machine.

4. The Attack Process Step-by-Step

1

Gathering Info: Researching the target on social media or search engines.

2

Fake Identitity: Creating a fake story (e.g., "I'm from the Bank Support Team").

3

Gaining Trust: Acting friendly or urgent to lower the victim's guard.

4

The Trick: Persuading the victim to share passwords, OTPs, or click a link.

5

The Payoff: Using that stolen information to steal money or take over accounts.

5. What They Gain

  • Stealing Money
  • Social Media Access
  • Identity Theft
  • Installing Malware

6. Damage Caused

  • Financial Loss
  • Privacy Invasion
  • Emotional Stress
  • Account Takeovers

7. Roles in Social Engineering

Attackers

The "engineers" who create the traps.

Victims

Individuals like students or employees.

Service Providers

Banks/Apps where our data is stored.

Security Teams

The professionals who protect the systems.

8 & 9. Awareness and Impact

Why Training Matters: Technology can't always block a human liar. Training helps you "see through" the masks and say NO to attackers.

For Individuals

Lost savings, stolen photos, and digital embarrassment.

For Organizations

Huge fines, loss of customer trust, and major data leaks.

10. Defense Strategies

Verify Identity

Call back the official number before sharing info.

Use MFA

Add a second layer of security to your accounts.

Filter Spam

Let AI block suspicious messages for you.

11. Ethical and Legal Issues

Crime and Punishment: Using social engineering for theft or unauthorized access is a crime. Attackers face heavy jail time and fines. As students, you have a Digital Responsibility to respect others' privacy and use your skills for good.

12. Top Safety Practices

Never share passwords or OTPs
Think before clicking any link
Verify emails from unknown senders
Use unique passwords + 2FA
Keep your phone/apps updated
Educate your friends and family
