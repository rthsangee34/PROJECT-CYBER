What is a DDoS Attack?
A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal traffic of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming it with a flood of Internet traffic.

The Botnet factor:
Attackers often use "botnets"—networks of compromised devices (PCs, IoT gadgets)—managed remotely to launch these massive floods.

🛠️ DDoS Mitigation Tips

  • • Anycast & CDN: Distribute traffic across global servers to soak up loads.
  • • Rate Limiting: Limit how many requests one IP can make per second.
  • • Cloud Scrubbing: Use 3rd party services to 'clean' traffic before it hits you.
  • • Monitoring: Set up alerts for sudden spikes in bandwidth usage.
