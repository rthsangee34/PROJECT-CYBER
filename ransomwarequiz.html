What is Ransomware?
Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts your files, making them inaccessible. Attackers then demand a ransom payment (usually in Bitcoin) to provide the decryption key.

How it spreads:
Most often through phishing emails, malicious attachments, or unsecured Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) ports. Once inside, it can spread across an entire network within minutes.

🛡️ Ransomware Prevention

  • • Immutable Backups: Keep offline backups that can't be encrypted.
  • • Patch Management: Keep your OS and software updated.
  • • Email Filtering: Use advanced tools to block malicious attachments.
  • • No Ransom: Paying the ransom does not guarantee file recovery.
